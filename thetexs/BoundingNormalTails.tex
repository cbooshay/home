\section*{Bounding Normal Tails \begin{small} \begin{color}{gray} \\
(\today) \end{color} \end{small}}

If $Z$ is a standard normal random variable, the tail probability $\ds P(Z>a)$ is given by 
\[
  \int_a^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-x^2/2}\, dx
\]
A crude bound for this can be had by observing
\begin{equation} \label{bound}
 \int_a^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-x^2/2}\, dx \leq
 \int_a^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \lp \frac{x}{a} \rp e^{-x^2/2}\, dx = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi} a e^{a^2/2}}
\end{equation}
The middle integral can be evaluated explicitly via the substitution $\ds u = x^2/2$. 

The approximation error--the difference between the two sides of (\ref{bound}), and the relative error--the approximation error divided by the correct value, i.e., the left side of (\ref{bound})--both appear to behave well.
